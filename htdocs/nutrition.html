Nutrition

[noo-trish-uh n, nyoo-]

Noun
The act or process of nourishing or of being nourished. The science or study of, or a course of study in, nutrition, especially of humans. The process by which organisms take in and utilize food material. Food; nutriment. The pursuit of this science as an occupation or profession.

Origin
Equivalent to Latin past particle of nūtrīre which means to feed.

The PFC provides plants with a controlled disease-free and pest-free environment during the growing phase. Additionally, PFC harvests are not subjected to the nutrient draining processes utilized to store and ship food. These are just two ways that the PFC enables plants to achieve their true expression and yield food at its peak freshness, texture, color, and nutritional value.


For more information, view Caleb Harper’s TED Talk on how the PFC not only helps us learn the nutritional language of plants, but also targets higher aspirations such as diminishing global hunger.
